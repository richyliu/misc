New Troop Website

A fresh look for our troop's main communication method

Posted by Richard Liu on May 19, 2016

For years our troop has been using outdated technology for our website. It has been mostly unchanged since it first launched back in 2006 with the help of Perry Ting. Although it has recieved many small updates over the years, it was clearly lagging behind in terms of clarity, usability, and beauty.

The brand new troop website will be released in July of 2016, featuring a brand new look based on industrial standards (Bootstrap 3) as well as updated information. The website will no longer be a non-functional, static piece of "furniture", but instead will revolutionize the way our troop communicates.

The Many Features

The most exciting feature the new troop website will deliver is a searchable, offline directory. This will help scouts and parents find each other more effectively, instead of searching through old emails.

The website will also include a calendar, where scouts and parents can view future events and plan accordingly. Calendars can only be edited by admins to ensure that the information is up to date and accurate.

In addition, the website will include many pages of information to help younger scouts know the ins and outs of our troop. It will contain information to help new scouts join, the ranking system, as well as current troop jobs.

Planned Feature

In order to release the troop website as quickly as possible, we had to leave out many helpful features, such as offline web app, event pages, and a login system.

Event pages are slated to be released in January of 2017. It will allow SiC to edit a web page and publish information such as the location of a campout, headcounts, and agendas. This New technology will radically change the way our troop communicates. Instead of digging through old emails, looking for headcounts spreadsheets, campount locations, or updated agendas, scouts and parents can simply check the event page.

Offline web apps are currently in development and should be released in July of 2016. Offline web apps will allow scouts and parents to view directory and event pages information while offline. It will also be integrated to appear just like an app downloaded from the app store. It will only support iOSTM initially. Details AndroidTM support will be announced later this year.

Beta Testing

If you are interested in Beta testing the troop website, please contact me at bsa485@gmail.com. Beta testing will be open from June 1 to June 15. Once the website is live, it will be available at t485.org
