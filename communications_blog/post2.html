Best Labor Day Ever

A break from the yearly Emigrant hikes

Posted by Richard Liu on May 7, 2017

For years our troop has been going to Emigrant Wilderness for our annual Labor Day Backpacking. Now, I don't have anything against Emigrant, but the trek has become dull and monotonous. Recently Star scout and SiC Jacob Nie has announced his vision for the future of Labor Day Backpacking

The Big Change

The change to the traditional Labor Day Backpacking will not only be focused on location, but also on group sizes. This year we will be going to various locations in the Sierras. Jacob has yet to announce specific locations, but he promises it will be "unlike any you've seen".

OK, as you all know, the past Labor Day Backpacking routes have been pretty boring. That is going to change. I have planned 6, thats right, more than 2, routes in the Sierra Nevadas. There will be fantastic views unlike any you've seen before!
—Jacob Nie, May 1 Troop Meeting

In a private interview with Jacob, he has revealed one of the six routes for the hike. "We definitely have to go to Yosemite", he said. He has also promised to work closely with AiCs to ensure the appropriate permits and reservations are made on time.

Should I Go?

This question should really be a no-brainer, but for anyone still on the edge, you should definitely go! This trip is a once in a lifetime oppurtunity and you definitely don't want to miss out. Although Jacob has yet to reveal the hike routes, I have great confidence in his planning abilities. He previously planned Mother and Son campout in New Brighton Beach, which turned out to be a huge success.
